What we are analyzing and why

This is a sample analysis based on performance data pulled from Motion for Botox Cosmetic's FB Feed Awareness placements in Q1 2026 (January–March) only. It is intended to illustrate the analytical approach and the type of creative insights this framework can surface — not to draw definitive conclusions. A full analysis would incorporate all placements (IG Feed, IG Stories, IG Reels), all funnel stages (Awareness, Consideration, Conversion), and the complete date range once that data has been consolidated by the media team.

Within this sample, we use the available data to understand what is working creatively and why — with the goal of surfacing insights and learnings that can guide both the optimization of existing assets and the development of new ones.

Spend share (% spend) is our starting point. Within a campaign, Meta allocates budget toward the assets it determines are performing — so which ads are receiving spend, and how consistently, tells us something meaningful about creative traction over time. But spend share alone does not tell us why an asset is working. Two ads can receive similar spend for very different reasons, and understanding that difference is where the creative insight lives.

To get beneath the spend data, we look at two creative performance signals. The first is Thumbstop — which the data confirms is the primary signal driving Meta's spend allocation in this dataset. The second is Thruplay CTR — not because it broadly correlates with spend across all assets, but because the assets receiving the most spend tend to show strength on both. That pattern is what makes the divergence between the two charts meaningful: an asset that is strong on thumbstop but drops on Thruplay is revealing a gap between what caught the algorithm's attention and what actually held the audience's. That gap is where the creative insight lives, and where the next brief should focus.

Chart A · Hook power
Thumbstop rate

The percentage of people who stop scrolling when the ad appears in feed. This measures the strength of the opening frame — the first 1–3 seconds that determine whether a viewer pauses or keeps scrolling. High thumbstop means the creative is visually arresting enough to interrupt behavior. It is a necessary but insufficient condition for performance: an ad that stops the scroll but fails to hold attention delivers impressions without impact.

Chart B · Hold power
Thruplay CTR

The percentage of people who watch the video to completion and then click through. This is the signal Meta's algorithm weighs heavily in Awareness placements — because completion behavior is a strong downstream predictor of purchase intent. An asset with high Thruplay CTR tells the algorithm that the creative not only caught attention but sustained it and converted passive viewing into active interest. This is the metric that earns algorithmic spend.

Key takeaways
Hook and hold are opposite forces  ·  r = -0.798
The strongest relationship in the dataset is a strong negative one. Ads with high thumbstop tend to have low 15s/3s retention and vice versa. The creative techniques that interrupt the scroll are not the same ones that sustain attention. Hook and hold need to be briefed and solved as separate creative problems.
Thumbstop drives both spend and clicks  ·  r = +0.570 / +0.620
Thumbstop is the most connected metric in the dataset. It positively correlates with both spend allocation and CTR link click. Meta is backing scroll-stopping creative, and that same quality is also motivating viewers to click through. This validates thumbstop as a genuine top-of-funnel performance signal, not just a vanity metric.
Thruplay CTR is an independent intent signal  ·  r = +0.524 with CTR
Thruplay CTR and CTR link click are moderately correlated. Ads that hold to completion also tend to drive clicks. But Thruplay CTR has a negligible relationship with spend (r = +0.099), meaning Meta is not yet systematically rewarding completion behavior in its spend allocation. The algorithm is spending on thumbstop, but completion is the better predictor of downstream intent.
The Andromeda gap  ·  r spend/thru = +0.099
Spend correlates meaningfully with thumbstop (+0.570) but barely with Thruplay CTR (+0.099). Meta is allocating budget primarily based on scroll-stopping power, even though completion behavior is the stronger predictor of purchase intent. Assets that hold attention without a strong hook are being systematically underfunded.
Male vs. Female  ·  Thruplay is the key gap
Across thumbstop, CPM, CTR and retention, Male and Female assets perform nearly identically. The one meaningful gap is Thruplay CTR: Male ads average 24.91% vs. 22.99% for Female. Male creative is holding attention to completion at a higher rate. Worth investigating in the next brief cycle whether this reflects narrative structure, message clarity, or audience behavior.
